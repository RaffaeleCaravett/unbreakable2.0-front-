Let's analyze now the best food you can eat

Meat

Meat is one of the single best foods on the planet. It has been demonized for so long, but the reason is that it is often been served associated with nitrites and nitrates, that are carcinogen, taked alone, meat is no dangerous, instead has the best bioavailable protein for muscle growth, function and synthesis. It is full of Vitamins, Minerals, top quality protein.

Veggies

Veggies are one of the best food of the planet too. They are high in fiber, are also full of minerals, and contain chlorophyll, that as strong antioxidant properties, detoxify, clean the blood, and prevent many illness. The only things to remember is that in the modern world, most of the veggies contains pesticides and herbicides. So we need to choose, biologic and organic veggies.

Wild-caught salmon

Rich in omega-3 fatty acids, especially EOA and DHA that together support heart and brain health, promoting cognitive functions, improving the decision taking process and so many other benefits, while being an excellent source of high-quality protein. Free from artificial additives and pollutants often found in farmed varieties, wild-caught salmon provides essential vitamins and minerals, and increase overall well-being. It is also good for the skin, with his saturated fats. The only thing that matters is that need to be wild caught, you know it by its color, should be pink and not orange like mosts salmon in the market.

Eggs

Pasture-raised eggs are a top choice for health as they come from hens that have access to outdoor spaces and a natural diet. These eggs are nutritionally superior, containing higher levels of omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins (such as vitamin D), and antioxidants compared to conventionally raised eggs.

Cheese

Cheese is a nutrient-dense food that offers various health benefits. It is an excellent source of high-quality protein, essential for muscle repair and maintenance. Additionally, cheese is rich in calcium, promoting strong bones and teeth. Certain varieties of cheese, especially those made from fermented milk, may contain probiotics that support a healthy gut microbiome, contributing to digestive well-being. Moreover, cheese provides essential vitamins and minerals, including vitamin B12, phosphorus, and zinc. These nutrients play vital roles in energy metabolism, bone health, and immune function.

Fruit

Fruits are a nutritional powerhouse, offering an array of health benefits. Packed with essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, fruits contribute to overall well-being. They provide a rich source of dietary fiber, promoting digestive health and helping to maintain a healthy weight. The antioxidants found in fruits help combat oxidative stress, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases. Fruits are also a natural source of vitamins like vitamin C, crucial for immune function and collagen synthesis.

Kefir

Kefir, known for its probiotic richness, is a fermented dairy product that promotes gut health. It contains a diverse array of beneficial bacteria, contributing to a balanced microbiome and supporting digestion. Probiotics in kefir may also enhance immune function and improve nutrient absorption.

Dark chocolate

Dark chocolate, when consumed in moderation, offers a range of health benefits. Rich in antioxidants like flavonoids, dark chocolate has been associated with improved heart health by promoting healthy blood flow and reducing blood pressure. Additionally, it may positively impact mood and cognitive function due to its content of compounds like serotonin precursors and caffeine.

Water

Water, essential for life, plays a vital role in maintaining overall health. Proper hydration supports bodily functions such as digestion, nutrient transport, and temperature regulation. Water helps flush toxins from the body, aids in joint lubrication, and promotes healthy skin. Staying adequately hydrated is crucial for optimal physical and mental well-being.

And ... that's not even everything !

Infact , even if those tips can help improve overall health, and contribute to promote longevity, and prevent various diseases there's still something to mention when we talk about nutrition: Intermittent Fasting

Intermittent fasting (IF) has been associated with various health benefits, including effects on hormones, cellular repair, and healing processes. Here are some of the potential benefits in these specific areas:

  1. Insulin Sensitivity:

    2. IF can improve insulin sensitivity, aiding in the regulation of blood sugar levels. This is particularly beneficial for individuals dealing with or at risk of type 2 diabetes.

  2. Human Growth Hormone (HGH) Production:

    3. IF is linked to increased secretion of human growth hormone, crucial for growth, metabolism, and muscle strength.

  3. Cellular Repair and Autophagy:

    4. Fasting promotes autophagy, a cellular cleaning process removing damaged cells and components, contributing to cellular repair and rejuvenation.

  4. Inflammation Reduction:

    5. IF may reduce chronic inflammation associated with diseases like cardiovascular conditions and neurodegenerative disorders.

  5. Gene Expression:

    6. IF can influence the expression of certain genes, potentially contributing to longevity and protection against diseases.

  6. Brain Health:

    7. IF is associated with increased production of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), supporting neuron growth and maintenance, potentially enhancing cognitive function.

  7. Enhanced Fat Burning:

    8. During fasting, the body relies on stored fat for energy, leading to increased fat burning and improved body composition.

  8. Oxidative Stress Reduction:

    9. IF may help reduce oxidative stress, the imbalance between free radicals and antioxidants linked to aging and various diseases.

  9. Improved Blood Lipid Profile:

    10. IF can positively impact cholesterol levels, reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

  10. Longevity:

    11. Some animal studies suggest that intermittent fasting may extend lifespan, though further research is needed to confirm this effect in humans.
